Maria KeïtaShe remains the first Nigerian woman to hold a post as a journalist at a time when the profession was exclusively reserved for men. From 2003 to 2006, Mariama Keïta held the position of President of the Higher Council for Communication (CSC), the body responsible for regulating the country's media.

Oluwaseun Ayodeji OsowobiDirector of Stand to End Rape Initiative (STER), she is responsible for conducting advocacy and project implementation and ensuring survivors of gender-based violence receive medical, legal, and psycho-social services.

Mariama Gamatié BayardShe was a consultant in developing countries, she worked on the status of women. She founded the Democratic Rally of Nigerian Women (RDFN).

Fatou DjiboShe founded the women's organization of the Union des Femmes du Niger (UFN), of which she became the president. She asserts that a country's development cannot be complete without the emancipation of women and that their enslavement must be condemned.
